2022 Draft Grades

Diarrhea has interrupted this write-up too many times to count. It’s only been my fault on one occasion. I probably shouldn't have had those three McDoubles after the draft on Sunday. Almost every other time has been Owen. I sit down to write, he starts crying, I pick him up to feed him, and a few ounces later, he blows out the side of his diaper and I lose all will to live. I feel like we just got out of the drive-thru, with my team pitching a mini-tent in my pants. How is it Saturday?

With each season that goes by, time picks up speed it seems, and that’s especially the case in this household. Days filled with a baby’s mustard colored shit covering my clothes, a cranky wife who needs literally any hobby, and a workload that makes me miss 80-hour work weeks at South + Pine has me desperately seeking the escape of fantasy football season. Scouring the waiver wire for upside, sending out a dozen trade requests a day, listening to every podcast under the sun… my dick is hard as we speak.

And then I look at my post-draft roster and I go limp. This will not be my year. 

But before I get to that, everyone else will get their grades. Am I going to research what each player ate for breakfast and where they went to elementary school like in years past? Absolutely not. I’ve got a few hours to work with here before Corin comes home from dinner with a friend, drunk off her ass on espresso martinis, to tell me she’s feeling icky and has a headache tonight. (Editor’s note: I was very wrong and doubted the power of espresso martinis!) I literally have a baby strapped to my chest right now because he’s being extra needy. Hell, I’ve got Erik waxing poetic about write-ups when the longest thing he’s written in a decade is a company-wide email delivering malware to everyone’s inboxes. Don’t expect War and Peace here.

At the end of the day, all of this – the write ups, the league itself, Thanksgiving Eves spent risking ligaments at the ripe old age of 32 – is FTB forever. Girlfriends turn to fiancées to wives. Our homes get more crowded. Our jobs become more hectic and demanding. With every year that passes, even without that beautiful trophy, I grow more grateful we still have this league as the little oasis in the desert to bring us together. I love you all, and wish your teams nothing but the absolute worst through January.

I’m grading out of 100 this year because it’s easier for me. I’ll take no questions on my reasoning. I haven’t slept more than 4 hours straight since July. As always these are completely subjective and biased. Without further ado, in draft order…

Guys I fucked up. This is arguably my worst draft ever, and I can’t sugar coat it. Let me get some of the excuses out of the way now.

  1. No sleep, I have a baby. Father of 1.
  2. It was really hot where I was sitting. I couldn’t think straight or see all of my cheat sheets.
  3. I was gassy from eating all that quiche and breakfast casserole.
  4. I overdid it with the adderall and had three cups of coffee. I was tweaking.
  5. Jay printing out his rankings from a desktop version of ESPN really threw me off.
  6. Steve kept calling out all of my targets before I could pick.

I was always leaning towards taking Lamar with my first round pick. He’s in a contract year and generally has performed better with a chip on his shoulder. I didn’t want any of the second tier of RBs at that price and drafting a safe WR at 6 seemed like a third place move. But what if CMC actually fell? What if Herbie dropped in my lap somehow? Paralyzed by that thought, I figured keep Trey Lance and hope he pops despite all of the negative training camp reports. With that I’d have my two QBs or I’d at least still have a projected top ten QB. Then what? It took me one round to tilt. Gimme all the QBs, I’ll take Hurts please. Then Aaron Jones as my RB1 even though he’s shown he’s fringe top 10 at best…  Then trusting Mike Evans’ hamstrings to get him enough separation before Brady is sacked… Then Tee Higgins with the likes of Sutton and Waller on the board… To cap off the tilt, we go with BREECE HALL as the RB2. The Jets o-line is a mess, they’re a bad team, and after a rocky preseason he's in a committee with Michael Carter. Awful all around. I tried righting the ship by picking exclusively RBs in murky committees in hopes of hitting the lottery. I forgot to draft a defense and will likely lose to Erik the first week because of it. Dawson Knox is my TE over Mark Andrews. I’ll never forgive myself for this. Prepare to be sick of me with all the trade requests. 67/100
